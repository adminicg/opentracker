Mobile Augmented Reality Hardware Setup - Current State

by Ivan Viola and Matej Mlejnek.

Description

The Mobile AR Hardware Setup consists of iGlasses for stereoscopic AR vision, Philips Web Cam for optical tracking (both mounted on the helmet) and Wacom Tablet and Pen for the user interaction. Wacom Tablet and Pen are optically tracked by the camera using AR Toolkit to estimate their relative position and orientation according to the user. There is one marker (8x8 cm) mounted on the Tablet and five markers (5x5 cm) on the Pen. These five markers are located on a cube (6^3 cm) at the end of the Wacom Pen.

The Setup also consists of two Intersense InterTrax2 sensors used for the body and head orientation estimation, forming so called "Body Stabilized System" - the world coordinate system depends on the body orientation. The one, which is tracking the body orientation, is located in the backpack, the another on the head, mounted on a helmet. All devices are plugged to a laptop in a backpack weared by the user.

The system has no absolute coordinate system - consists only of a single user, that "takes his applications with him".

Due to configuration flexibility, after adding a referential marker (of known position), the user can interact in absolute coordinate system. This enables him to collaborate with another mobile and/or stationary users.

 
 

Configuration

The Mobile Augmented Reality uses Studierstube (Collaborative Augmented Reality) as Virtual Environment. The connection to the hardware is managed by OpenTracker (Open Software Achitecture for Reconfigurable Tracking) using XML for tracking configuration. The relative position of the pen, according to the camera, is given by five ARToolKitSource nodes. To estimate the correct coordinates of the pen tip, each tracker's base point (originally positioned in the middle of the marker) has to be transformed (translated and rotated) to the pen tip using EventVirtualTransform. These values are merged to single event. When the pen is touching the pad, the position can be estimated more precisely from the pad position (ARToolKitSource) and position of the pen on the pad (WacomGraphireSource). This position event is prefered from the previous one because of the higher precision (Selection node). Because of lack of the button when the pen is away from the pad, the keyboard keys (ConsoleSink) can be used as a button substitution (This is just a temporary solution, the wireless button will come soon).

The pad is equipped with one marker (ARToolKitSource) that is tracked for the position and orientation estimation (transformed by EventTransform and EventVirtualTransform).

The InterTrax in the backpack (InterSenseSource) estimates the global orientation of the body - orientation of the whole HW setup. The another InterTrax, that is mounted on the helmet, estimates the viewing direction of the user. The resulting orientation of both events has influence on relative orienation of pad, pen and viewpoint (DynamicTransformation).
